Pork Sinigang Recipe

Sinigang is a soup dish from the Philippines characterized by its sour and savory taste. It's iconic sour taste is often associated wwith the use of a tamarind base though variatons exist that use other sour fruits and leaves as the souring agent.

The dish's name is derived from the nominalized form of the Tagalog verb sigang which means "To stew".

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 lbs pork belly
  • Half bunch spinach
  • 1.5 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 6 pieces string beans cut in 2 inch length
  • 1 piece tomato quartered
  • 2 pieces chili
  • 0.5 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 quart water
  • 1 piece onionsliced
  • 1 piece taroquartered
  • Half pack of sinigang mix good for 2 liters of water
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the pot and put-in the cooking oil
  2. Saut√© the onion until its layers separate from each other
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown
  4. Put-in the fish sauce and mix with the ingredients
  5. Pour the water and bring to a boil
  6. Add the taro and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender
  7. Put-in the sinigang mix and chili
  8. Add the string beans (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes
  9. Put-in the spinach, turn off the heat, and cover the pot. Let the spinach cook using the remaining heat in the pot.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
